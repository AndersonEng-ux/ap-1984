I — The argument

1.1 What the system is for

Anderson Psomas works for buyers whose own standing rides on the work holding up — a permit review, a state regulator, a lender's underwriting, a corporate EHS audit. Oil-and-gas and industrial project managers, owners of construction firms, county engineers, the lender behind a land deal. None of them buy on emotion. Every one of them is handing the deliverable to someone stricter than themselves, and they need it to survive that handoff.

To that buyer, a stamped engineering document reads as trustworthy and a sales pitch reads as a warning. The system exists to make every surface the firm ships — the credibility site, PPC, proposals and reports, Phleet, the quoting tool — read like the document, not the pitch.

The restraint is the credibility argument, not a house style. Most firms try to look impressive. The winning move for this buyer is to look like the work that survives scrutiny: severe, verifiable, unhurried. Every law in this document is downstream of that single decision.

1.2 Capture vs. credibility — the two machines

A firm needs two different machines, and they are not the same machine.

A capture machine pulls in transactional and consumer work — SEO, PPC, turn-key services (septic, retaining walls, residential). It is measured in leads. It can carry urgency, repetition, and conversion mechanics.

A credibility machine wins the work that is decided on whether the firm is serious — pursuits, industrial, energy, public-sector. It is measured in trust. It cannot carry a single ounce of marketing theater without losing the buyer it is built for.

This system is the credibility machine. It also governs the capture surfaces (the PPC intent profiles live inside it) — but the capture profiles invert the physics on purpose (see §8.5). The point of naming the two machines: a value that is correct on a PPC landing page (one repeated CTA above the fold) is a defect on the About page, and the system encodes that difference rather than averaging it away.

1.3 Why enforced, not documented

Most brand standards are a PDF and good intentions — followed until the deadline, then not. This system can't be ignored, because the build enforces it. A hardcoded color fails the lint. A contrast failure stops the build. A page that drifts from the canon doesn't ship.

The standard is not a request to designers. It is a gate in the pipeline. That is the difference between a brand guideline and brand infrastructure — and infrastructure is the thing that survives a deadline, a new hire, an agent, and a rebrand.

1.4 The cost of drift — what this forecloses

Drift is not hypothetical. Without a system, every surface is a one-off: a proposal, a landing page, and an app screen built the same week don't match — different type, different spacing, different rules, each rebuilt by hand. Consistency depends on one person remembering, and memory doesn't scale across offices, agents, or a relaunch.

The failure modes the system forecloses, drawn from a real ungoverned site:

  • A header phone link that won't dial, and typos shipped to the homepage — because nothing checks markup. *(Closed by htmlcheck.mjs.)*
  • A brand that can't decide on its own name — logo says one thing, domain and About page say another. (Closed by the canonical registration line and the navy dial.)
  • Stats that drift across the same page — "20 years" in one card, "24+" in the next; "95% retention" here, "95% satisfaction" there. (Closed by the verifiable-claims voice law.)
  • A value-word grid — Expert Oversight, Tailored Solutions, Agile Communications. (On the kill-list.)
  • SEO filler and off-brand backlinks in the footer. (The funnel the system refuses to build.)

None of that ships because anyone decides it should. It ships because nothing checks. The system checks.

1.5 The three properties

The brand line — severe, verifiable, unhurried — is not decoration. Each word is an operational instruction.

  • Severe — zero radius, monochrome-plus-one, hairline structure, no ornament. The visual signal of a drafting sheet, not a brochure. Carried by: radius 0, the grey ramp, the one red.
  • Verifiable — numbers over adjectives, every claim links to its registry, mono marks data you could check. Carried by: the mono-is-data law, the verify-strip, the voice laws.
  • Unhurried — type at 500 not 600, motion that reveals rather than performs, whitespace that separates rather than boxes. Confidence reads as calm. Carried by: NHG 500, the motion law, the elevation restraint.

II — The laws

2.1 The six hard laws

Six laws, ratified by eye and enforced by build. Break one and the lint or the contrast gate fails.

01 · Radius is always 0.
--ap-radius: 0 is the only radius token; base.css force-zeros every element, even radios.
Why: rounded corners are the universal default of consumer/SaaS friendliness. Zero radius is the opposite signal — drafting-sheet, engineering-document, severe. It is the single most visible carrier of the brand, which is why it is locked (AP 1984).
Enforced by: lint L3 (no-radius).

02 · Type is NHG Medium 500, tracking 0.
Neue Haas Grotesk Display/Text at 500 (never 600), 400 for deks and body. IBM Plex Mono is DATA only.
Why: 600 reads as assertive, marketing-bold; 500 holds presence without shouting — "unhurried." Letterspacing display type is a fashion tic that dates; tracking 0 is timeless and document-grade.
Enforced by: lint L5 (no-weight-600), L6 (no-negative-tracking).

03 · Monochrome-plus-one.
Ink #111111 plus ONE permit red #B32A2E. One red action per screen; red links always underlined.
Why: a single chromatic accent is what makes the red mean something. The moment a second accent enters, the red stops being a signal and becomes a color. Restraint is what gives the one red its authority.
Enforced by: lint L1 (no-hex), review (the action budget).

04 · Navy is structure, never a voice.
Pacific Blue #0C3350 lives in the lockup, rules, and shelves; intensity scales with formality (the navy dial). No navy text token exists.
Why: navy as a text/voice color would dilute monochrome-plus-one and compete with the red. Confined to structure, it signals formality and the Psomas endorsement without becoming a second accent.
Enforced by: lint L4 (navy-structure-only).

05 · Charts are ink-first.
Graphite outlines, ONE red marker (the figure that matters), a navy dashed reference line. Never a rainbow.
Why: color in a chart must carry meaning. A rainbow says everything matters, therefore nothing does. Ink draws; one red marker points; the eye goes straight to the figure that matters.
Enforced by: review (the one-marker rule).

06 · Three registers retune one canon.
Editorial (warm-white #FCFCFA, 16px) · app (white workbench, 15px, motion ≈ 0) · print (816×1056, 10px floor, prints white).
Why: a website, a workbench app, and a printed proposal have different jobs and different physics. One canon retuned by register keeps them one brand without pretending they are one medium.
Enforced by: the semantic layer (data-register).

2.2 The judgment rules

These can't be linted — they are review-enforced. They matter as much as the six.

  • One red action per screen. A primary button exists only where its action belongs to the page; at most one ghost (Back); red links underlined.
  • Mono = data only. Fees, dates, IDs, licenses, coordinates. Mono never sets a sentence; letterspaced caps for single-line labels only.
  • Charts ink-first, one marker. The categorical palette only when hue IS the data — one per real category.
  • Imagery real or absent. Stock never stands in for a named person or place; the fallback is an ink schematic marked NOT TO SCALE.
  • States are mandatory. Every listable view ships empty, loading, and error states; state changes get a toast and an undo; errors are text, never color alone.

III — Color

3.1 Philosophy

Warmth lives in the ground, the permit red, and photography — never in type. Ink is #111111, always. The grey ramp is neutral (the stones were neutralized from taupe to luminance-matched greys). Navy is structure only. The six data hues are categorical-chart-only. Reach for the semantic layer, never raw color: components touch var(--ap-text-primary), never --ap-color-ink directly.

3.7 Color combination rules — the manual

  • Semantic, never raw. var(--ap-text-primary) for type, var(--ap-surface-page) for grounds, var(--ap-border-soft) for hairlines. The raw --ap-color-* layer exists only so the semantic layer can chain to it. Components touch raw color zero times.
  • One red per screen. The single action budget. A second red action means the first one stopped being the action.
  • Red links are always underlined. Color is never the only signifier (WCAG 1.4.1).
  • Red on navy and red on ink fail for text — no component composes them for type. Structural-only navy keeps this from happening by accident.
  • Navy never sets type. No navy text token exists. Navy enters a surface through the dial (§6.2), as structure.
  • Warmth never enters type. It lives in the warm-white ground, the red, and photography. Type is ink, neutral, always.
  • The grey ramp is purposeful, not a gradient to pick from. Each rung has one job. decor-grey is the trap — large display only.

IV — Typography

4.1 The three faces

Two families plus a data face. Each carries exactly one job.

  • Neue Haas Grotesk Display · 500 — headlines. Presence without the marketing shout of 600. "Unhurried."
  • Neue Haas Grotesk Text · 400 — body and deks, 16px / 1.55. The reading voice.
  • IBM Plex Mono — DATA only. Fees, dates, IDs, licenses, coordinates. Never prose, never multi-line caps.

Why mono is locked to data. Mono signals this is a value you could check — the typographic form of "verifiable." Set a sentence in mono and that signal is gone. Reserve it, and every mono string reads as a fact. This is the "verifiable" property made visible. Tracking is 0; weight is never 600.

4.4 Type combination rules — the manual

  • Display sets headlines, Text sets prose, Mono sets data. Never cross them. A headline in Text reads weak; a fee in Text reads unverifiable; a sentence in Mono reads broken.
  • Sentence case everywhere except single-line mono labels (letterspaced caps).
  • The all-caps law. In a key/value block, keys are single-line caps and may not wrap; values are sentence case and may wrap. Caps are for labels, never for content.
  • Mono never multi-lines as caps and never sets running prose. One line, one value.
  • 500, never 600. Tracking 0, never negative. Both are lint-enforced (L5, L6). Bold-by-weight is not available because "louder" is not a tool this system offers.
  • Body is 16px / 1.55; the reading measure caps at 720px (§5.3) so lines never run too long to scan.

5.3 Layout rules

  • 1280px max content width (.ap-container).
  • 720px reading measure — prose never runs wider, so lines stay scannable.
  • Auto-grid caps at 4 columns (.ap-autogrid) so card rows never sprawl.
  • Hairlines and 2px ink rules structure everything. Whitespace separates; hairlines divide; 2px rules are the structural beats (section heads, KPI strips). Shadows are reserved for hover and overlays only.

5.4 Structure rules — the manual

  • Whitespace separates; cards never "highlight." Reach for space before a border, a border before a fill.
  • The eye-director budget: pick three. Ink underlines, big ink numbers, the one red. A surface that uses all of these everywhere directs the eye nowhere.
  • 2px ink rule = a structural beat. Section heads sit on it with a mono counter. Use it to mark the page's rhythm, not as decoration.

VI — The registers and the navy dial

6.1 The three registers

One canon, three tunings, switched by data-register on a wrapping element. The semantic layer re-points; components don't change. Set it and everything inside retunes.

RegisterGroundBase sizeMotionSurfaces
editorial (default)warm-white #FCFCFA16pxopacity + rise revealswebsite · PPC · proposals
app data-register="app"white workbench15px≈ 0quoting tool · Phleet
print data-register="print"prints white10px floornone816×1056 proposals/reports

Why three. A page you read, a tool you operate, and a sheet you print have different physics. Editorial can breathe and reveal; the workbench must be still and dense; print has a fixed page and a 10px floor. One canon keeps them one brand; three registers keep them honest to their medium.

6.2 The navy dial

Navy intensity scales with formality, switched by data-navy-tier — structure only, never text. The dial is how the Psomas endorsement gets louder as the stakes rise.

TierNavy presenceUse on
acquisitionink rules only (navy quiet)PPC / acquisition pages
corenavy rules + footer shelfthe credibility site (default)
proposal+ full navy endorsement bandformal proposals

Why a dial. The same navy that would be too formal on a PPC landing page is exactly right on a proposal. Rather than two brands, one navy whose intensity follows the surface's stakes. Set it on <html> or a section.

6.3 How retuning works

Set the register and tier on a wrapper. The semantic tokens re-point to register-appropriate raw values; components consume the semantic layer and never know which register they're in. This is why a rebrand is a token edit, not a redesign: change the canon once, every register and every surface follows.

VII — Motion, elevation, iconography, data-viz

7.1 Motion

Entry is opacity + a small rise only — 24px at 600ms in editorial, overlays at 200ms, ease cubic-bezier(0.22, 1, 0.36, 1), stagger ≤ 3. Exits are instant. App motion ≈ 0. Durations: 150 / 200 / 600.

The one transform exception: button press scale(0.98), instant — owner-ratified. Everything else moves by opacity and rise, never by transform.

Why so little. Motion that performs reads as marketing; motion that reveals reads as confident. Reveals must enhance an already-visible default and always carry a prefers-reduced-motion alternative — nothing is lost when motion is off.

7.2 Elevation

A deliberately tiny 4-stop scale. Whitespace separates, hairlines structure, shadows appear only on hover and overlays. Reach for elevation by job, not by taste.

StopJob
elevation-0base / resting
elevation-1hover lift
elevation-2dropdowns, toasts, menus, popovers
elevation-3dialogs

7.3 Iconography

A hand-drafted fiducial set on a 24px grid: 1.5px ink stroke (1.25 at 16px), square terminals, miter joins, currentColor, no fills. Drafting-instrument language — north arrows, benchmarks, monuments, section callouts, alongside the standard UI and discipline glyphs.

Rules: every glyph is a <symbol viewBox="0 0 24 24"> — size variants scale, never clip. No fills, no rounding (the geometric lock applies to icons). Never cross-file <use href="other.svg#id"> — without the host page's stroke rules it degrades to solid-black fills. A missing glyph gets drafted on the same 24px / 1.5px grammar — never imported from Lucide or a third-party pack. New glyphs join via the Birth Gate (§9.4).

7.4 Data visualization — the one-marker rule

Ink draws the page; you reach for one marker. Charts default to ink; color is a deliberate act spent on the one figure the reader must see. If a color can't say why it's there, it comes off.

Chart roles: ink/graphite #5A564E (the default — outline bars, trend lines) · marker #B32A2E (the ONE figure that matters) · reference navy #0C3350 (baselines/targets, dashed, never competing) · gridline #BDBDBD (quiet) · label #4A4A4A (mono axis text).

VIII — Composition and strategy

8.1 The component inventory

50 components, grouped. The generated catalog (system/catalog.json) is the registry of record — tokens consumed, states, a11y, usage rules, and a canonical snippet per component. The groups:

  • Actions — button, arrow link, quickform
  • Structure — masthead, footer shelf, breadcrumb, section head, nav sheet, icon, card grammar, big door, engineer card, figbox, accordion, tabs
  • Data display — ledger row, spec table, table, KPI strip, benchmark, Q&A, verify strip, gantt, tag/chip/badge, pagination, charts
  • Forms & feedback — form field, checkbox, radio group, fieldset, file upload, combobox, toast+undo, empty state, dialog, notification, skeleton
  • Application — menu, tooltip, popover, progress, steps, command palette, app shell + rail, topbar, activity feed, theme toggle
  • Print — print page, print spec + fee table, redline grammar

8.2 The eye-director budget

Three eye-directors exist: ink underlines, big ink numbers, the one red. Pick three — meaning, use them sparingly enough that each still directs. A KPI strip gets one red number. A card gets one director. Everything competing for the eye means nothing wins it.

8.3 How to build a page

Pages aren't designed — they're composed against a profile. The method:

  1. Pick the intent profile for the page type (§8.4). No profile = a page you shouldn't build yet.
  2. Select components from that profile's Composes list, in its declared order.
  3. Set the register and navy tier for the surface's stakes.
  4. Honor the kill-list — the things that must never appear on that page.

8.4 The 13 intent profiles

Each profile names who visits, the one question they resolve, what composes the page, and what never appears. The pressure gradient (§8.5) governs CTA intensity across them.

8.5 The pressure gradient

On the credibility site, CTA pressure decreases as page importance increases. About Us runs near zero; Contact runs none (the decision is already made). The PPC family inverts this completely — one CTA, repeated, above the fold. Same tokens, same components; the intent profile flips the physics. This is the capture-vs-credibility split (§1.2) encoded as composition.

IX — Governance and the build

The system is maintained by one human and many AI agents. Everything below is machine-checkable; the build and lint catch drift before it ships.

9.1 The gates (violations fail the build)

9.2 The judgment rules (review-enforced — no lint can catch these)

The §2.2 rules live here too, because they are the part a machine can't verify: one red action per screen; mono = data only; charts ink-first one marker; imagery real or absent; states mandatory. These are caught in review, not by the gate — which is why they are written down.

9.3 Changing a value

Edit tokens/tokens.json — never a component file, never a page. Log it in decisions.log. Run the gates. A rebrand is a token edit. The locked decisions (§2.3) change only by owner ruling plus a logged receipt.

Read these, in order, before proposing a change: tokens/tokens.json (the canon — every token has value, type, provenance, contrast assertions) · system/catalog.json (the component registry) · system/intents.json (the composition layer) · decisions.log (why every value is what it is — most "improvements" were already litigated).

9.4 The Module Birth Gate

New components are demand-grown, never speculative. A component enters the catalog only when a real surface needs it — not because a system "should" have one. An exclusion (something the system deliberately doesn't have) stops being justified the day a surface demands it, and then it enters through the Birth Gate. This is why the system stays small: 50 components, each earning its place.

9.5 Why it survives its author

The question to ask of any one-person system is what happens when that person isn't there. Here, nothing breaks. Every value carries its provenance; every decision carries its reasoning in decisions.log; the reference is generated from source and gated by build. An agent reads the canon, the catalog, and the log, and works inside the gates. It is an asset that outlives its author, not a dependency on him — hand it to another operator and an agent, and the gates hold the line.
